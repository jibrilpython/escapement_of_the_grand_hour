# Escapement of the Grand Hour 

Description

Escapement of the Grand Hour is a mobile application for collectors of horological antiquities, historians of precision timekeeping, and preservers of micro-mechanical engineering heritage, dedicated to cataloging the vintage watch and clock escapement mechanisms that regulated the flow of time before the era of modern quartz movements and digital silicon oscillators.
Systematize your collection of the beating hearts of timepieces—from early iron verge-and-foliot setups to complex marine detent mechanisms, from precision English lever escapements to rare double-wheel chronometer modules that flawlessly divided the seconds inside grand regulator clocks.
Create a digital archive of heavy industry by recording the instrument type, manufacturer, temperature range, era, and the specific steel mill, foundry, or ceramic kiln each specimen once calibrated.

## Bespoke Object Fields (Model "Horological Mechanism"):

•	Chrono Matrix Index: A unique, system-generated cryptographic horological tracking string (e.g., EGH-TIME-1029-LEVER-W).

•	Escapement Architecture: The specific geometry of the mechanical drop-and-impulse configuration (e.g., Verge and Foliot, Deadbeat Anchor, Cylinder, Pivot Detent, English Left-Ankle Lever).

•	Artisan Hallmark: Fictionalized clockwork guilds and old-world master workshops (e.g., AetherTime Chronometrics, Meridian Gear & Balance, Sovereign Pendulum Works).

•	Lift Angle Metrics: The precise mechanical rotation angle of the balance staff during the impulse phase, measured strictly in degrees (e.g., 42°, 51.5°).

•	Design Frequency Profile: The intended operational vibration rate of the regulatory assembly, noted in vibrations per hour or Hertz (e.g., 18,000 VPH, 2.5 Hz).

•	Pallet Metallurgy & Material: The composition of the tiny locking pallets that interface with the escape wheel teeth (e.g., Synthetic ruby inserts, polished flint, oil-hardened carbon steel, brass sleeves).

•	Escape Wheel Tooth Count: The absolute physical tooth density machined onto the primary drive gear wheel.

•	Physical Proportions: The absolute diameter of the escape wheel and main plate layout, along with total mass in grams.

•	Preservation Soundness: The physical state of the mechanical pivots (e.g., Pivot groove wear, hairspring concentricity, pallet face pitting).

•	Horological Ground Zero: The fictionalized regional tower clock cabin, master watchmaker's bench, or railway timing house where the mechanism kept watch (e.g., Historic northern maritime beacon tower, old city municipal hall vault, forgotten mountain valley station).
